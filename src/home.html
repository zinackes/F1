THE DRIVERS

Formula 1 drivers, the epitome of racing excellence, ascend through tiers like F4, F3, and F2 before reaching the elite F1 grid. These 20 drivers hold the coveted Super Licence, a racing equivalent of a driver's license. This exclusive credential demands a track record of success in lower categories, ensuring that only the most skilled and accomplished drivers compete at the pinnacle of motorsport.

In the world of Formula 1, teams are the lifeblood of the competition, each with its own unique identity and history. Renowned manufacturers and iconic racing names, such as Ferrari, Mercedes, and Red Bull Racing, invest heavily in cutting-edge technology and top-tier talent to field competitive cars. Team dynamics play a pivotal role, as engineers and strategists collaborate with skilled drivers to optimize performance on the track. The fierce competition among teams adds an extra layer of excitement to the championship, with each squad vying for victory in the pursuit of the Constructors' Championship, a prestigious title that crowns the best team in the season.
